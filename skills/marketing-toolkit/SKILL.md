# Marketing Toolkit

Foundation-first marketing skill covering strategy, copy, conversion, and growth.

## Foundation: Product-Marketing Context

Before any marketing work, establish context:

1. **Product/Service**: What do you sell? What problem does it solve?
2. **Target Customer**: Who buys this? Demographics, psychographics, pain points
3. **Unique Value Prop**: Why choose you over alternatives?
4. **Market Position**: Premium, mid-market, budget? Niche or broad?
5. **Current Channels**: Where do customers find you today?
6. **Business Goals**: Revenue target, growth rate, key metrics?

## Copywriting

### Headlines
- **Problem-agitate**: "Tired of [pain point]?"
- **Benefit-driven**: "[Desired outcome] in [timeframe]"
- **Social proof**: "Join [number] [people] who [achieved result]"
- **Curiosity gap**: "The [unexpected thing] about [topic]"

### Landing Page Structure
1. **Hero**: Headline + subheadline + CTA + hero image
2. **Problem**: Describe the pain the visitor feels
3. **Solution**: How your product solves it
4. **Social Proof**: Testimonials, logos, case studies, numbers
5. **Features/Benefits**: What they get (benefits > features)
6. **Objection Handling**: Address common concerns
7. **CTA**: Clear, specific action with urgency

### Email Sequences

#### Welcome Sequence (5 emails)
1. **Welcome + Quick Win** (Day 0): Deliver immediate value
2. **Story** (Day 1): Your origin story, why you built this
3. **Value** (Day 3): Educational content, build trust
4. **Social Proof** (Day 5): Case study or testimonial
5. **Offer** (Day 7): Clear CTA to convert

#### Cold Email
```
Subject: [Specific observation about their business]

Hi [Name],

I noticed [specific thing about their company/role].

[One sentence about what you do and the result].

[Specific, relevant social proof - one line].

Would it make sense to [specific low-commitment ask]?

[Name]
```

### Ad Copy Framework (PAS)
- **Problem**: Call out the specific pain
- **Agitate**: Make them feel the cost of inaction
- **Solution**: Present your product as the answer

## Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO)

### Page CRO Checklist
- [ ] Single, clear CTA above the fold
- [ ] Value proposition in first 5 seconds
- [ ] Social proof near the CTA
- [ ] Form fields minimized (ask only what's needed)
- [ ] Mobile-optimized experience
- [ ] Loading speed under 3 seconds
- [ ] Trust signals (security badges, guarantees, testimonials)
- [ ] No distracting navigation on conversion pages

### Signup Flow CRO
- Reduce steps (ideal: 1-2 steps)
- Use progressive profiling (ask more later)
- Show progress indicators for multi-step
- Pre-fill where possible
- Offer social login options
- Show what happens after signup

### Pricing Page CRO
- Anchor with highest plan first (or highlight recommended)
- Use odd pricing ($49 vs $50)
- Show annual savings prominently
- Feature comparison table
- FAQ section below pricing
- Money-back guarantee

### A/B Testing Framework
1. **Hypothesis**: "If we [change], then [metric] will [improve] because [reason]"
2. **Metric**: Primary KPI + guardrail metrics
3. **Sample Size**: Calculate minimum for statistical significance
4. **Duration**: Run for at least 2 full business cycles
5. **Analysis**: Statistical significance > 95%, practical significance matters too

## Growth Engineering

### Referral Program Design
- **Incentive structure**: Two-sided rewards (giver + receiver both benefit)
- **Timing**: Ask for referral after customer achieves first success
- **Friction**: One-click sharing (pre-written messages, unique links)
- **Tracking**: Attribution system for referral source

### Launch Strategy

#### Pre-Launch (4-6 weeks before)
- Build waitlist with landing page
- Create teaser content on social media
- Reach out to potential early adopters personally
- Prepare launch assets (screenshots, demo video, press kit)
- Line up beta testers and early reviews

#### Launch Week
- Day 1: Product Hunt / Hacker News launch
- Day 1-3: Email all waitlist in batches
- Day 1-7: Social media blitz (different angles daily)
- Day 3: First case study / use case post
- Day 7: Wrap-up post with results, next steps

#### Post-Launch (2-4 weeks after)
- Collect and publish testimonials
- Write detailed case studies
- Address common objections in content
- Iterate on messaging based on customer feedback

### Content Marketing
- **Pillar content**: Comprehensive guides (2000+ words) targeting high-volume keywords
- **Cluster content**: Supporting articles linking back to pillars
- **Distribution**: Share each piece across 5+ channels
- **Repurposing**: One long-form piece becomes tweets, LinkedIn posts, email, slides

## Customer Research

### Interview Framework
1. **Context**: "Tell me about your role and what you're working on"
2. **Problem discovery**: "What's the hardest part about [task]?"
3. **Current solutions**: "How do you handle [problem] today?"
4. **Impact**: "What happens when [problem] isn't solved?"
5. **Desired outcome**: "In an ideal world, how would this work?"

### Survey Design
- Max 5-7 questions for completion rates
- Use rating scales (1-5) for quantitative data
- Include one open-ended question for qualitative insights
- Segment responses by customer type/stage
- Close with NPS or CSAT question

## Social Media Content

### Post Types (80/20 Rule)
- **80% Value**: Educational, entertaining, thought-provoking
- **20% Promotional**: Product updates, offers, CTAs
